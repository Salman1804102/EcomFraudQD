
1) This work proposes a zero-shot learning-based user feedback analysis on software product reviews—specifically, Browser reviews scraped from the Google Play Store. This is the first known attempt to classify a large volume of user reviews from different applications on the Play Store in an unsupervised manner. Therefore, this work can serve as a benchmark for future research on software product review analysis, going beyond the traditional focus on e-commerce reviews and helping to identify critical issues in software application.

2) In place of traditional clustering methods for unsupervised learning, this study also promotes the usage of LLMs for text classification and categorization in unsupervised manner. Moreover, the results show that LLMs can be efficient and effective for such tasks, successfully setting a benchmark in this field.

3) Additionally, it is shown that combining BERTopic with manual labeling significantly helps LLMs assign accurate labels to unlabeled text samples. This finding can encourage further research into improving LLM performance using such hybrid techniques.

4) This work also achieves a strong trade-off between specificity and generalization of models. Typically, highly domain-specific models do not generalize well, and generalized models lack domain focus. This study presents a balanced approach, which may guide future research in designing models that are both domain-relevant and broadly applicable.

5) Finally, by utilizing external related datasets for model selection and prompt engineering, the study introduces a method that could be used in other tasks by the researchers in this manner. 

6) This study will also guide in explainability analysis of the model's prediction in near future, a powerful and trending research field called Explainable-AI (XAI).

1) Previous studies have shown that traditional ML, DL, and transformer-based models perform competitively with LLMs on standard datasets. This study builds on that foundation by evaluating several state-of-the-art LLMs on a dataset of 5,000 Samsung Browser reviews scraped from the Google Play Store.

2) The classification results are validated through comparison with both manually annotated labels and BERTopic-generated labels. This benchmarking confirms the consistency and reliability of the outcomes. The strong performance of DeepSeek-14B under this framework further supports the conclusion that it is a suitable zero-shot learning model for this task.

3) In addition, the dataset and annotations were reviewed and verified by two domain experts, which adds an extra layer of credibility and ensures the validity of the results presented.

4) The inclusion of diverse datasets and consistent evaluation metrics further enhances the robustness of the findings.
