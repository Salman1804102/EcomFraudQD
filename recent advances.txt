User reviews play a critical role in shaping product development and brand perception in e-commerce and app ecosystems. Manual analysis of these reviews is time-consuming and inefficient, especially for large-scale platforms like the Google Play Store. In this study, we propose a generalized zero-shot learning (ZSL) based multi-label complaint classification framework to analyze negative user reviews of the Samsung Internet Browser. To determine the label we utilized both manual process and BERTopic to find topic or label from the dataset. We scraped 10k negative user reviews of ‘Samsung Internet Browser’ with ratings below 3 given by the user. We evaluated several state-of-the-art opensource LLMs—including GPT-3.5, Claude, DeepSeek, and LLaMA—on existing labeled datasets to determine the most effective model and then applied the best one on our collected dataset in order to classify the reviews in multi-label way. DeepSeek outperformed other models and we utilized deepseek for our task.
